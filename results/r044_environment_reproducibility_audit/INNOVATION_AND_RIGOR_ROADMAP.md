# R044 Innovation And Rigor Roadmap

Date: 2026-07-02

## Scientific Position

The project should preserve the current honest position:

- R021 rejects LV-VoI scale3 superiority under cost matching.
- R022 and R024 show that two lightweight repairs remain dominated.
- R023 traces diagnose intervention spending patterns but do not overturn R021.

This position is not a weakness if the paper is framed as a diagnostic protocol
for robotic HIL-RL trigger evaluation.

## Innovation To Strengthen Now

Near-term novelty should come from the protocol and artifact discipline:

1. Cost-matched random-family baselines as a required comparator.
2. Repeated checkpoint evaluation as a default reporting rule.
3. Intervention-start trace diagnostics as a mechanism-analysis tool.
4. Stop/redesign gates that prevent unsupported trigger-superiority claims.
5. Registry-driven evidence tables and source-audited citations.

This package can be presented as a reusable evaluation methodology for
automation and robotic manipulation systems.

## What Not To Do Yet

- Do not add another small LV-VoI heuristic simply to obtain a positive line in
  the table.
- Do not claim robotics generalization from Stack while Stack remains dominated
  by the strong no-online matched baseline.
- Do not cite smoke runs or toy trends as paper evidence.
- Do not add literature from memory.
